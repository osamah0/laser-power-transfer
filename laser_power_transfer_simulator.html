Core equations used in this simulator
1. BEER-LAMBERT ATMOSPHERIC ATTENUATION
P(R) = P(0) · e−σR · ηλ
P(R) = beam power at distance R  |  P(0) = initial beam power
σ = attenuation coefficient (km⁻¹) — depends on weather
ηλ = wavelength transmission factor (0.88–0.97 depending on λ)
2. RAIN ATTENUATION (empirical)
αR = a · Jb
J = rainfall rate (mm/h)  |  Ruby laser: a = 0.16 ± 0.04, b = 0.74 ± 0.12
Note: rain attenuates less than fog because forward Mie scattering keeps energy on-axis.
3. LPC TEMPERATURE CORRECTION
ηLPC(T) = ηbase · [1 − 0.001 · (T − 25)]
Temperature coefficient: −0.1% / °C  |  Voc coefficient: −1.6 mV / °C
Validated for InGaAs/GaAs cells over 20–100°C range (±0.5% stability over 216 h)
4. END-TO-END EFFICIENCY
ηtotal = ηWPE · ηATM · (1 − ηtrack-loss) · ηLPC(T)
ηWPE = laser wall-plug efficiency  |  ηATM = atmospheric transmission
Typical range: 3–20% (ground); up to 45% optimised (space / vacuum)
5. LPC MULTI-JUNCTION VOLTAGE STACKING
Vtotal = N · Vjunction   |   Itotal = Ijunction / N   |   Pohmic ∝ I²·R
N = number of stacked p/n junctions (tested: up to 20 for GaAs)
Reduces ohmic losses at high irradiance (>150 W/cm²). GaAs 12-junction: η = 61% at 40 W, 811 nm.
Key safety thresholds — IEC 60825-1 / ANSI Z136.1
